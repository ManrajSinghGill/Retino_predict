What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It’s caused by damage to the blood vessels of the retina due to high blood sugar levels. Early detection and treatment are crucial to prevent blindness.

At RetinoPredict, we use cutting-edge AI to analyze retinal images and assist in early detection.

Stages of Diabetic Retinopathy

  • Mild Nonproliferative Retinopathy: Small areas of swelling in the retina’s blood vessels.
  • Moderate Nonproliferative Retinopathy: Progression of retinal blood vessel blockage.
  • Severe Nonproliferative Retinopathy: Blood vessels are blocked, signaling the growth of new ones.
  • Proliferative Retinopathy: The advanced stage where abnormal blood vessels grow, threatening vision.
